My Mac OS X Setup for Web Development

Posted on 2015-11-25

Yesterday I demonstrated the ways of building simple websites and creating more complex single-page-application (SPA) in the SSG Seasonal Research & Tech Workshop. During and after the event, I was asked heavily about my setup for doing web development on my Macbook, so that I decided to write a post here to share my setup for web development on my Macbook.

iTerm2

Although the Terminal.app of Mac OS X is powerful enough for regular uses, I still prefer to use iTerm2. You can take a glance at the power of iTerm2 on its feature page.

Sublime Text 3

Sublime Text 3 is a powerful programming text editor. Although I sticked to Vim for several years, I eventually switched to Sublime Text since it (1) is fast – at least not significantly slower than Vim for me ; (2) provides a great bunch of plugins – most of the plugins that I used in Vim are either ported or cloned (un)officially; and (3) looks great. :-)
